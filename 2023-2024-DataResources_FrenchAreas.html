Toolset - available datasets on the French territories

Three kind of databases are available to draw analysis on the French territories:

Mobility Datasts mainly related to the Origin-Destination matrices with distinctions according to transportation modes and trip reasons. Two kind of data are available: real-world data from field observations and surveys and data resulting from simulation tools . Supplementary datasets are also available and are more related to specific types of mobility: logistic, urban, periurban.
Urban Planning Data and maps relating to information on the location of planning urban planning components, whether for green spaces, car parks, housing for green spaces, parking lots, housing, transport and water hydraulic network, etc. Almost all of the maps available for download are in ShapeFile (.shp) format. This format can be opened with the free geographic information software QGIS ( to download here ). Note that you need to use the same projection system, for example WGS 84 (EPSG:4326), to map several files in the same configuration.
Economic Data relating to investment plans for autonomous vehicles and shuttles and autonomous shuttles, including associated infrastructure software development and hardware, etc. This data will enable costing and feasibility studies of feasibility of project scenarios in economic terms. economic terms. These data are currently difficult to obtain, given that that they mainly come from private players. Nevertheless bibliographical references, such as the 17th dataset, provide fairly detailed economic assessments.


The following database list is non exhaustive and only provide a set of support materials. It is not required to use it to complete the course, it is only a toolset.

/!\ WARNING : Les matrices OD produites par le LAET sont exprimées en Boucles de Motif, tandis que les matrices proposées par le LICIT sont exprimées en trajets unitaires. Une Boucle de déplacements est composée d'un ensemble de trajets constituant un cycle. Ainsi, une boucle peut se composer de la succession de déplacements: Domicile-Travail-Ecole-Achat-Domicile, mais être résumée à une seule boucle étiquetée par les motifs principaux Domicile-Travail. Les trajets, dits secondaires, sont alors ignorés. La quantité de trajets issus de cette matrice est donc sous-estimée, mais les tendances en termes de répartition spatiale sont signficatifs. 		- CSV

About the French territories and some definitions


  • IRIS Zones. The IRIS zones correspond to an official geographical division defined by INSEE (Institut National de la Statistique et des Études Économiques). Each administrative district contains one or more IRIS. Their exact delimitations can be viewed in the gate geoPortail.
  • Enquêtes Ménage Déplacement 2015. The latest mobility produced by the urban planning agency for the Lyon metropolitan area. It is available on the UrbaLyon website lien.
  • Métro E. A project for a 5th TCL metro line, currently under study, was the subject of a public consultation between March and May 2019 (link). Further information is available here.
  • Navettes autonomes dans le territoire de Décine-Meyzieu-Grand Parc. Two autonomous shuttle projects were launched during the summer of 2019 in this region: the Mia shuttles (link) and Groupama shuttles (link).
  • Covoiturage pour les territoires de Saint-Quentin-Fallavier-Villefontaine et de l'ouest lyonnais. Carpooling is a growing mobility practice in Lyon's urban and suburban area. in and around Lyon. Although difficult to quantify, it is becoming increasingly visible thanks to dedicated websites. More information on this subject is available on the following references: l'enquête 2018 suite aux assises du covoiturage organized by the Agence Régionale Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Énergie Environnement, the user survey produced by the Grand Lyon between 2013 and 2016, that is based on end-users of webportal covoiturage-grandlyon.com , the UrbaLyon's report produced in 2014. Regarding Saint-Quentin urban district, an urban document, called plan de déplacements urbains, is provided by Communauté d’Agglomération Porte de l’Isère (CAPI).
  • Autoroutes dans les territoires de l'ouest lyonnais. Sections of the A6 and A7 motorways were decommissioned in 2016 and became metropolitan highways in 2020 (dossier de concertation du grand Lyon). The objective is to foster the development of new mobility practices in the west of Lyon: strengthening public transport links public transport links, large-scale car-pooling, development of bicycle paths, etc. A major project for Greater Lyon's ring road, known as the "Anneau des Sciences", is the subject of much controversy, is currently under construction, with a time horizon of 2030. à 2030 (project steps).
